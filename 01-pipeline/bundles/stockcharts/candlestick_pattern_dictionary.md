# Candlestick Pattern Dictionary

The StockCharts Candlestick Pattern Dictionary provides brief descriptions of many common candlestick patterns.

### Abandoned Baby <a href="#abandoned_baby" id="abandoned_baby"></a>

A rare reversal pattern characterized by a gap followed by a Doji, which is then followed by another gap in the opposite direction. The shadows on the Doji must completely gap below or above the shadows of the first and third day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/pNh5J6uWiMwIwSn9JTPT" alt="Abandoned baby candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Dark Cloud Cover <a href="#dark_cloud_cover" id="dark_cloud_cover"></a>

A bearish reversal pattern that continues the uptrend with a long white body. The next day opens at a new high, then closes below the midpoint of the body of the first day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/rjGq8wPWcaaqflTibcIp" alt="Dark cloud cover candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Doji <a href="#doji" id="doji"></a>

Doji form when the open and close of a security are virtually equal. The length of the upper and lower shadows can vary, and the resulting candlestick looks like either a cross, inverted cross, or a plus sign. Doji convey a sense of indecision or tug-of-war between buyers and sellers. Prices move above and below the opening level during the session but close at or near the opening level.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/h5OoAaI8StpfjxhggAqG" alt="Doji candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Downside Tasuki Gap <a href="#downside_tasuki_gap" id="downside_tasuki_gap"></a>

A continuation pattern with a long, black body followed by another black body that has gapped below the first one. The third day is white and opens within the body of the second day, then closes in the gap between the first two days, but does not close the gap.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/nVyCxJ9XtDuN8lHn6cVF" alt="Downside Tasuki gap candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Dragonfly Doji <a href="#dragonfly_doji" id="dragonfly_doji"></a>

A Doji where the open and close price are at the high of the day. Like other Doji days, this one normally appears at market turning points.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/M7jqOTF5pjixXecF23Bw" alt="Dragonfly doji candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Engulfing Pattern <a href="#engulfing_pattern" id="engulfing_pattern"></a>

A reversal pattern that can be bearish or bullish, depending upon whether it appears at the end of an uptrend (bearish engulfing pattern) or a downtrend (bullish engulfing pattern). The first day is characterized by a small body, followed by a day whose body completely engulfs the previous day's body and closes in the opposite direction of the trend. This pattern is similar to the [outside reversal chart pattern](/table-of-contents/glossary/glossary-o.md#outside_reversal_day), but does not require the entire range (high and low) to be engulfed, just the open and close.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/S2qq4IXy7knhK6us31rG" alt="Engulfing candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Evening Doji Star <a href="#evening_doji_star" id="evening_doji_star"></a>

A three-day bearish reversal pattern similar to the Evening Star. The uptrend continues with a large white body. The next day opens higher, trades in a small range, then closes at its open (Doji). The next day closes below the midpoint of the body of the first day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/wUwKsNUBmByjy6vcljlR" alt="Evening doji start candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Evening Star <a href="#evening_star" id="evening_star"></a>

A bearish reversal pattern that continues an uptrend with a long white body day followed by a gapped up small body day, then a down close with the close below the midpoint of the first day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/fuXtm7yQFdiJLIrPdWxC" alt="" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Falling Three Methods <a href="#falling_three_methods" id="falling_three_methods"></a>

A bearish continuation pattern. A long black body is followed by three small body days, each fully contained within the range of the high and low of the first day. The fifth day closes at a new low.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/zQx9HHz2RMbR8USrQaU7" alt="Falling Three Methods candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Gravestone Doji <a href="#gravestone_doji" id="gravestone_doji"></a>

A doji line develops when the Doji is at, or very near, the low of the day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/1XO4FV3ixFg9xTBBP5tm" alt="Gravestone doji candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Hammer <a href="#hammer" id="hammer"></a>

Hammer candlesticks form when a security moves significantly lower after the open but rallies to close well above the intraday low. The resulting candlestick looks like a square lollipop with a long stick. If this candlestick forms during a decline, it is called a Hammer.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/xuH07PU29F6VU85qOyjA" alt="Hammer candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Hanging Man <a href="#hanging_man" id="hanging_man"></a>

Hanging Man candlesticks form when a security moves significantly lower after the open but rallies to close well above the intraday low. The resulting candlestick looks like a square lollipop with a long stick. If this candlestick forms during an advance, it is called a Hanging Man.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/Z5GiVroiW9NmQTBQZPUl" alt="Hanging man candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Harami <a href="#harami" id="harami"></a>

A two-day pattern that has a small body day completely contained within the range of the previous body, and is the opposite color.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/6PoUL1leE3fdRZdAXvek" alt="Harami candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Harami Cross <a href="#harami_cross" id="harami_cross"></a>

A two-day pattern that's similar to the Harami. The difference is that the last day is a Doji.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/9slFQFVPoUKa4RStTxCp" alt="Harami cross candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Inverted Hammer <a href="#inverted_hammer" id="inverted_hammer"></a>

A one-day bullish reversal pattern. In a downtrend, the open is lower, then it trades higher, but closes near its open, therefore looking like an inverted lollipop.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/WIH1DfmgXvdWqB6oYlCs" alt="" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Long Body / Long Day <a href="#long_body_long_day" id="long_body_long_day"></a>

A large price move from open to close, i.e., the length of the candle body is long.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/SNnAJQgj3Y17EUdiMdq8" alt="Long body/long day candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Long-Legged Doji <a href="#long-legged_doji" id="long-legged_doji"></a>

This candlestick has long upper and lower shadows with the Doji in the middle of the day's trading range, clearly reflecting the indecision of traders.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/H6lv0gpjCLz9wnTuakz6" alt="Long-legged doji candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Long Shadows <a href="#long_shadows" id="long_shadows"></a>

Candlesticks with a long upper shadow and short lower shadow indicate that buyers dominated during the first part of the session, bidding prices higher. Conversely, candlesticks with long lower shadows and short upper shadows indicate that sellers dominated during the first part of the session, driving prices lower.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/sVDuSXndjLWJRYpDTPj5" alt="Long shadows candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Marubozu <a href="#marubozu" id="marubozu"></a>

A candlestick with no shadow extending from the body at the open, close, or both. The name means close-cropped or close-cut in Japanese, though other interpretations refer to it as Bald or Shaven Head.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/sZQbsYGsaeOUEvmm89cb" alt="Marubozu candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Morning Doji Star <a href="#morning_doji_star" id="morning_doji_star"></a>

A three-day bullish reversal pattern that's similar to the Morning Star. The first day is in a downtrend with a long black body. The next day opens lower with a Doji with a small trading range. The last day closes above the midpoint of the first day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/ilhgMWUcbKbU86RNgd0N" alt="Morning doji star candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Morning Star <a href="#morning_star" id="morning_star"></a>

A three-day bullish reversal pattern consisting of three candlesticks—a long-bodied black candle extending the current downtrend, a short middle candle that gapped down on the open, and a long-bodied white candle that gapped up on the open and closed above the midpoint of the body of the first day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/4b9oFvX8GQE4uqio3DCQ" alt="Morning star candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Piercing Line <a href="#piercing_line" id="piercing_line"></a>

A bullish two-day reversal pattern. The first day, in a downtrend, is a long black day. The next day opens at a new low, then closes above the midpoint of the body of the first day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/napLXOPjd8Mat1g0Es3k" alt="Piercing line candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Rising Three Methods <a href="#rising_three_methods" id="rising_three_methods"></a>

A bullish continuation pattern in which a long white body is followed by three small body days, each fully contained within the range of the high and low of the first day. The fifth day closes at a new high.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/rYrmB5kzxgmgr4FXlqFg" alt="Rising three methods candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Shooting Star <a href="#shooting_star" id="shooting_star"></a>

A single-day pattern that can appear in an uptrend. It opens higher, trades much higher, and then closes near its open. It looks just like the Inverted Hammer, except it's bearish.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/yzAYka53hriMt6tMLVeO" alt="Shooting star candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Short Body / Short Day <a href="#short_body_short_day" id="short_body_short_day"></a>

A short day represents a small price move from open to close, where the length of the candle body is short.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/VNYpdrKGJr5LTg8Sp5Ek" alt="Short body/short day candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Spinning Top <a href="#spinning_top" id="spinning_top"></a>

Candlestick lines that have small bodies with upper and lower shadows that exceed the length of the body. Spinning tops signal indecision.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/7idwAnJQaRqA9bUAumqR" alt="Spinning top candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Stars <a href="#stars" id="stars"></a>

A candlestick that gaps away from the previous candlestick is said to be in a star position. Depending on the previous candlestick, the star position candlestick gaps up or down and appears isolated from the previous price action.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/m7EgV1A3RFrDn8HtEuw8" alt="Stars candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Stick Sandwich <a href="#stick_sandwich" id="stick_sandwich"></a>

A bullish reversal pattern with two black bodies surrounding a white body. The closing prices of the two black bodies must be equal. A support price is apparent, and the probability for prices to reverse is high.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/nvVCmpRbel6PMa0CcHfT" alt="Stick sandwich candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Three Black Crows <a href="#three_black_crows" id="three_black_crows"></a>

A bearish reversal pattern consists of three consecutive long black bodies where each day closes at or near its low and opens within the body of the previous day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/Eb0MIuCYJ8NwUqEaTOOp" alt="Three black crows candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Three White Soldiers <a href="#three_white_soldiers" id="three_white_soldiers"></a>

A bullish reversal pattern consisting of three consecutive long white bodies. Each should open within the previous body, and the close should be near the high of the day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/pbtWErjumUvdSoSfEiC5" alt="Three white soldiers candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Upside Gap Two Crows <a href="#upside_gap_two_crows" id="upside_gap_two_crows"></a>

A three-day bearish pattern that only happens in an uptrend. The first day is a long white body followed by a gap open with the small black body. The gap remains above the first day after the close. The third day is also a black candlestick with a body larger than the second day and engulfs it. The last day's close is still above the first long white day.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/NH8FVHHw5GdxYFtcVtrI" alt="Upside gap two crows candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

### Upside Tasuki Gap <a href="#upside_tasuki_gap" id="upside_tasuki_gap"></a>

A continuation pattern with a long white body followed by another white body that has gapped above the first one. The third day is black and opens within the body of the second day, then closes in the gap between the first two days but does not close the gap.

<div align="left"><figure><img src="/files/KOvcQTzXedSs3alTxXGZ" alt="Upside tasuki gap candlestick pattern from StockCharts.com" width="225"><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

## Scanning for Charts With Candlestick Patterns <a href="#charts_with_current_candlestick_patterns" id="charts_with_current_candlestick_patterns"></a>

StockCharts.com maintains a list of stocks with common candlestick patterns on their charts in the [**Predefined Scan Results**](https://stockcharts.com/def/servlet/SC.scan) area. To see these results, [click here](https://stockcharts.com/def/servlet/SC.scan) and scroll down until you see the **Candlestick Patterns** section. The results are updated throughout each trading day.
